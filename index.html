Capturing Important Memories

One of the most meaningful functions photography holds is that it is a catalog of memories. Life moves quickly and moments pass fast. A photo can freeze time — it can hold emotion, highlight milestones, capture celebrations, and even represent simple, mundane moments.

Family gatherings, travel experiences, birthdays, graduations, and other personal events, are often valued and remembered through photographs. These memories can allow us to return to our past... and see the people we love, and where we were at that moment in time, while also representing a snapshot of how much we have all grown since. Improving Communication and Storytelling

With digital communications, visuals have replaced the importance of the written word. Photography allows people to convey stories and feelings that words do not.

A singular photograph can express sadness, joy, anger, love, or wonder without uttering a single word. For bloggers, journalists, influencers, and educators, photographs bring depth and emotion to convey a message in less time. On social media, compelling visuals can tell a more powerful story than a wordy caption or a few line status update.

Enhancing Mental Health and Mindfulness

Photographs can also offer mindfulness by way of self-care. Many people find photography a therapeutic and stress relieving activity. It can encourage people to slow down, take time and observe their environment and the present.

Photography can also promote creativity and give people an avenue to express themselves in visual form. Taken in our immediate environment, photographs can create a sensation of grounding. Photographs can help us appreciate beauty in the unremarkable, a ray of sun, a quiet street, or a child's smile.

Enhancing Personal Identity

Photographs reflect how we see the world, and how we see ourselves. In photographs, we develop personal narratives and develop more deeply our sense of personal identity. The photographs we take (and choose to keep or share) reflect our values, interests, and memories.

Likewise, personal branding today is also part of a visual medium. In this day and age, personal branding is heavily linked to visual content. From professional LinkedIn headshots, to curated Instagram feeds, photography plays a big role in determining how others see us.

Documenting Social and Cultural Awareness

Photography has long been utilized as a medium for pushing social change, documenting injustices, and chronicling social history. Powerful images have started protests, swayed public opinion, and changed the course of history.

Photography can encourage empathy by exposing individuals to other lifestyles, cultures, and struggles from all over the world. It can break down barriers and open an understanding that goes beyond words and borders.

Support Business, Marketing and Education

In business, photography can help a company or organization with marketing, branding, and selling its products or services. Good photography exists in websites, ads, and product packaging and the type of photos used can influence consumer choice. In the world of digital economy and accelerated consumerism, visual storytelling has become essential for a successful brand -- and can literally offer a brand an advantage in its competition versus another brand that has not.

In education, photography can make complicated ideas easier to convey. People who are visual learners can often better recall information by seeing it depicted in a photograph than by reading about it in a textbook. There is a multitude of examples, from science textbooks about plants and animals, to online video tutorials for arts and crafts.
